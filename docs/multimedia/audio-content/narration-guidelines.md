## üé§ Narration Techniques

### Pacing Guidelines

```markdown
## Content-Based Pacing

**Tutorial Content**: 140-150 wpm
- Slower pace for step-by-step instructions
- Allow time for listeners to process steps
- Include natural pauses between procedures

**Explanatory Content**: 150-160 wpm
- Standard conversational pace
- Comfortable for technical explanations
- Balanced information delivery

**Introductions/Conclusions**: 160-170 wpm
- Slightly faster for familiar territory
- Creates energy and momentum
- Engaging for attention grabbing

**Complex Technical Content**: 130-140 wpm
- Slower for dense information
- Extra pauses for comprehension
- Clear pronunciation of technical terms
```

### Emphasis & Intonation

```markdown
## Emphasis Patterns

### Key Terms
**Example**: "The DEDICATED SQL pool provides..."
- Slight emphasis on first mention
- Natural tone on subsequent mentions
- Clear pronunciation always

### Contrasting Concepts
**Example**: "Use dedicated pools for PRODUCTION, serverless for EXPLORATION"
- Emphasize both contrasted items
- Slight pause between concepts
- Rising then falling intonation

### Critical Information
**Example**: "This setting CANNOT be changed after creation"
- Strong emphasis on critical words
- Slower pace for important details
- Repeat if necessary

### Questions (Rhetorical)
**Example**: "Why does this matter?"
- Rising intonation at end
- Pause before answering
- Engaging, conversational tone
```

## üìù Script Interpretation

### Reading Techniques

```markdown
## Natural Delivery

**Avoid**:
- Reading in monotone
- Emphasizing every other word
- Robotic pacing
- Over-pronunciation

**Practice**:
- Conversational tone as if explaining to a colleague
- Natural breathing patterns
- Slight variations in delivery
- Authentic enthusiasm for content

### Technical Terms

**First Mention**: "Azure Synapse Analytics [PAUSE] is Microsoft's..."
- Clear, deliberate pronunciation
- Brief pause after term
- No over-emphasis

**Subsequent Mentions**: Natural integration
- Consistent pronunciation
- Normal conversational flow
- Confidence in delivery

### Acronyms

**First Use**: "We'll use the A-W-S [PAUSE] Amazon Web Services..."
- Spell out letters clearly
- Provide full name
- Brief pause between

**Later Use**: "A-W-S provides..." (no explanation needed)
```

## üéµ Working with Music & Sound

### Music Integration

```markdown
## Background Music Guidelines

**During Speech**:
- Volume: -30 dB to -35 dB below voice
- Style: Subtle, non-distracting
- Tempo: Matches content energy
- Fade: Smooth transitions (2-3 seconds)

**Between Segments**:
- Volume: -10 dB to -15 dB (more prominent)
- Duration: 3-5 seconds typically
- Purpose: Clear section separation
- Style: Consistent with brand

**Intro/Outro**:
- Volume: -3 dB peak (full presence)
- Duration: 10-15 seconds
- Fade: Professional in/out
- Recognition: Consistent branding
```

### Voice-Music Balance

```markdown
## Ducking Technique

When voice begins over music:
1. Music fades from -10 dB to -30 dB over 1-2 seconds
2. Maintains low level throughout speech
3. Fades back to -10 dB after voice ends (1-2 seconds)
4. Smooth, gradual transitions

**Audio Engineering Note**:
Use sidechain compression or manual automation for
professional ducking effect. Avoid abrupt cuts.
```

## üé¨ Recording Best Practices

### Pre-Recording Preparation

```markdown
## Before Recording Session

**Script Review** (30 minutes before):
- [ ] Read through entire script aloud twice
- [ ] Mark pronunciation questions
- [ ] Note emphasis and pacing cues
- [ ] Practice complex technical terms
- [ ] Identify natural breathing points

**Physical Preparation**:
- [ ] Hydrate well (room temperature water)
- [ ] Avoid dairy products (can cause mouth noise)
- [ ] Warm up voice (5-10 minutes)
- [ ] Avoid caffeine (can cause jitters)
- [ ] Clear throat gently if needed

**Environmental Setup**:
- [ ] Close doors and windows
- [ ] Turn off HVAC if possible
- [ ] Silence phones and notifications
- [ ] Remove noisy accessories (jewelry, etc.)
- [ ] Position microphone correctly (6-8 inches)
```

### During Recording

```markdown
## Recording Session Guidelines

**Microphone Technique**:
- Consistent distance: 6-8 inches from mouth
- Slight off-axis (avoid popping on plosives)
- Maintain position throughout session
- Use pop filter always
- Monitor levels continuously

**Performance Tips**:
- Stand if possible (better breath support)
- Gesture naturally (adds energy to voice)
- Smile when appropriate (audible in tone)
- Take breaks every 15-20 minutes
- Re-record unclear sections immediately

**Quality Checks**:
- Monitor audio through headphones
- Watch for clipping (red meters)
- Listen for mouth noises
- Check consistent volume levels
- Save backups frequently
```

### Common Issues & Solutions

```markdown
## Troubleshooting Vocal Issues

**Mouth Noises/Clicks**:
- Cause: Dry mouth, sticky saliva
- Solution: Sip water, eat green apple
- Prevention: Hydrate 30+ minutes before

**Sibilance (harsh "S" sounds)**:
- Cause: High-frequency emphasis, mic positioning
- Solution: Adjust mic angle, use de-esser in post
- Prevention: Position mic slightly off-axis

**Plosives ("P" and "B" pops)**:
- Cause: Direct air hitting microphone
- Solution: Use pop filter, adjust mic position
- Prevention: Speak across mic, not directly into it

**Inconsistent Volume**:
- Cause: Moving toward/away from mic
- Solution: Mark floor position, maintain stance
- Prevention: Focus on consistent mic distance

**Vocal Fatigue**:
- Cause: Extended recording without breaks
- Solution: Rest voice, hydrate, reschedule if needed
- Prevention: Limit sessions to 2 hours max, take breaks
```

## üìê Technical Specifications

### Audio Quality Standards

```markdown
## Recording Settings

**Sample Rate**: 48 kHz (broadcast standard)
**Bit Depth**: 24-bit (professional quality)
**Format**: WAV (uncompressed for editing)
**Channels**: Mono for voice (single narrator)

**Level Targets**:
- Peak Level: -6 dBFS (leaves headroom)
- Average Level: -18 dBFS to -12 dBFS
- Noise Floor: Below -60 dBFS
- Dynamic Range: 10-12 dB typical for narration

## Post-Processing Chain

Standard processing order:
1. **Noise Reduction**: Remove background noise
2. **EQ**: Enhance clarity (high-pass at 80Hz, presence boost)
3. **De-esser**: Tame sibilance
4. **Compression**: Even out dynamics (3:1 ratio, soft knee)
5. **Limiting**: Prevent peaks, maximize loudness
```

### Export Specifications

```markdown
## Final Output Formats

**Master Archive**:
- Format: WAV, 48kHz, 24-bit
- Compression: None
- Purpose: Future re-editing

**Production Master**:
- Format: WAV, 48kHz, 16-bit
- Normalization: -16 LUFS (podcast standard)
- Purpose: Final mixdown before distribution

**Distribution**:
- Format: MP3, 320 kbps CBR or V0 VBR
- Metadata: Complete ID3 tags
- Purpose: Public release
```
